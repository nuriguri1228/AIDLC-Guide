## Status

The Operations phase in the current AIDLC version is a **placeholder for future operational phases** including:

- **Deployment planning** — release strategy, canary / blue-green / rolling
- **Monitoring setup** — dashboards, SLOs, alert routing
- **Incident response** — runbooks, on-call rotation, postmortem templates
- **Maintenance workflows** — patching, dependency upgrades, capacity planning
- **Production readiness documentation** — go-live checklists, operational handover

## Why It's a Placeholder

The current AIDLC workflow concludes after **CONSTRUCTION → Build and Test**, which has been consolidated into the construction phase. Operations represents planned future development rather than implemented functionality.

## What This Means for You

When the AIDLC workflow completes the Build and Test stage, the workflow ends. Operational concerns (deployment, monitoring, incident response) are handled by your team's existing operational tooling and processes — not by AIDLC at this time.
